## üìã Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: We're a startup with 120 employees but less than $5M revenue. Do we need a commercial license?

**Yes.** If you have 100+ employees, you need a commercial license regardless of revenue.

However, we offer **startup-friendly pricing**. Contact us at license@bytelogic.studio to discuss options.

---

### Q2: We're a profitable company with $8M revenue but only 80 employees. Do we need a commercial license?

**Yes.** If your annual revenue exceeds $5M, you need a commercial license regardless of employee count.

---

### Q3: We're a non-profit organization with 100 employees. Do we need a commercial license?

**No.** Non-profit organizations, educational institutions, and research organizations can use DBX under the MIT License regardless of size.

Please contact us at license@bytelogic.studio to confirm your eligibility.

---

### Q4: We're using DBX in an open-source project. Do we need a commercial license?

**No.** Open-source projects can use DBX under the MIT License regardless of the organization's size or revenue.

---

### Q5: What if we exceed the thresholds mid-year?

If your organization grows beyond the thresholds (e.g., hiring your 100th employee or exceeding $5M revenue), you should obtain a commercial license **within 30 days**.

We understand growth happens! Contact us and we'll work out a fair transition plan.

---

### Q6: How do you enforce this policy?

We rely on **voluntary compliance**. Most organizations have legal and compliance teams that review software licenses, and they will identify the need for a commercial license.

However, we reserve the right to:

- Monitor public usage (GitHub, job postings, tech blogs)
- Request compliance audits
- Pursue legal action for willful violations

**Retroactive fees and penalties may apply for unauthorized commercial use.**

---

### Q7: Can we get a trial period?

**Yes!** We offer a **30-day free trial** for commercial licenses. Contact us at license@bytelogic.studio to get started.

---

### Q8: What if we're acquired by a larger company?

If your organization is acquired by a company that meets the commercial license criteria, you must obtain a commercial license **within 60 days** of the acquisition closing.

---

### Q9: Do contractors and part-time employees count toward the 50-employee threshold?

**Yes.** All workers who contribute to your organization's operations count, including:

- Full-time employees
- Part-time employees
- Contractors
- Temporary workers

---

### Q10: We're just evaluating DBX. Do we need a license?

**No.** Evaluation, testing, and proof-of-concept work do not require a commercial license.

However, once you deploy DBX in a production environment, you must comply with the license policy.
